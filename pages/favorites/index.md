<!-- BOOKS SECTION -->
<div id="books" class="tab-content" style="display: block;">
    <p class="tab-text-blurb">These are some of my favorite books of all time. I generally struggle with ranking favorites, so these are listed in no particular order. </p>
    <ul class="fav-list">
        <li class="fav-item">
            <img src="../assets/favorites/taintedcup_cover.jpg" alt="The Tainted Cup Cover" class="fav-img">
            <div class="fav-text">
                <h3>The Tainted Cup by Robert Jackson Bennett</h3>
                <p><em>The most recent addition to my favorites, The Tainted Cup takes place in a uniquely built fantasy world where humans can augment themselves using the blood of the massive leviathans that threaten the safety of the Empire. I love the world that Bennett has built, blending classical fantasy elements with new and creative ideas. I love our two main characters, Din and Ana, whose contrasting outlooks on life and work spark witty exchanges that make them both deeply endearing. Finally, I love the murder mystery aspect of the book. The story is always moving one step forward without leaving the reader confused or annoyed. Overall, this was an incredible read, and the second book did not disappoint either. </em></p>
            </div>
        </li>
        <li class="fav-item">
            <img src="../assets/favorites/wayofkings_cover.jpg" alt="The Way of Kings Cover" class="fav-img">
            <div class="fav-text">
                <h3>The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson</h3>
                <p><em>The Cosmere is an intimidating set of books to try and get into. However, the Way of Kings is a great first book and introduction to the series. I think that the typical Sanderson criticisms are valid—he struggles with dialogue, the Sanderlanche is unnecessary, and the plot is at times contrived. Despite this, I still think that the world that he creates is one of the most unique and amazing out of all the fantasy authors that I have ever read. Bonus points for Kaladin, Shallan, and Adolin being great characters. </em></p>
            </div>
        </li>
        <li class="fav-item">
            <img src="../assets/favorites/infinitejest_cover.jpg" alt="Infinite Jest Cover" class="fav-img">
            <div class="fav-text">
                <h3>Infinite Jest by David Foster Wallace</h3>
                <p><em>I read Infinite Jest in 10th grade for an author synthesis in English class. It was the most frustrating book that I had read up to that point. Instead of reading the entire book in one sitting, I had to read a few chapters at a time and pause to try and process what I had read. Despite this, it was one of the most rewarding books that I've read. To this day, the ideas that Wallace discussed remain relevant. Modern society struggles with overconsumption and addiction more than ever, and corporations seem to have endless new schemes to exploit this. </em></p>
            </div>
        </li>
        <li class="fav-item">
            <img src="../assets/favorites/babel_cover.jpg" alt="Babel Cover" class="fav-img">
            <div class="fav-text">
                <h3>Babel by R.F. Kuang</h3>
                <p><em>Babel is a novel with a unique magic system. Words translated into another language are used in pairs to make the world run. However, these pairs become less powerful as they become more well known, so academics are always on the hunt for more obscure languages to use. Kuang tackles concepts of colonialism and cultural appropriation through this novel. I really enjoyed the magic and I think that despite being surface level, her critiques were powerfully written. </em></p>
            </div>
        </li>
        <li class="fav-item">
            <img src="../assets/favorites/sixofcrows_cover.jpg" alt="Six of Crows Cover" class="fav-img">
            <div class="fav-text">
                <h3>Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo</h3>
                <p><em>Six of Crows is my favorite young adult book of all time. Each character brings something different to the team and the plot actually requires the use of each character and their skills. In addition to that, the Crows are just fun characters to read. The Crooked Kingdom is also a great sequel. </em></p>
            </div>
        </li>
    </ul>
</div>

(function() {
    const tabLinks = document.querySelectorAll('.tab-link');
    const tabContents = document.querySelectorAll('.tab-content');


    tabLinks.forEach(link => {
        link.addEventListener('click', function() {
            const targetTab = this.getAttribute('data-target');


            // Reset active states
            tabLinks.forEach(l => l.classList.remove('active'));
            tabContents.forEach(c => c.style.display = 'none');


            // Apply current active states
            this.classList.add('active');
            const matchingContent = document.getElementById(targetTab);
            if (matchingContent) {
                matchingContent.style.display = 'block';
            }
        });
    });
